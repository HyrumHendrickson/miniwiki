Social Sciences

Social Sciences
ScopeScientific study of human society, behavior, and institutions
Major disciplinesEconomics, Political Science, Sociology, Psychology, Anthropology
MethodEmpirical observation, surveys, experiments, comparative analysis, ethnography

The social sciences are the disciplines that apply scientific methods to the study of human society, behavior, and institutions. They seek to understand how individuals and groups act, how societies are organized, how power is distributed and contested, how economies function, and how cultures develop and change over time. Unlike the natural sciences, the social sciences often confront unique challenges of objectivity, value-laden subject matter, and the complexity of human agency, yet they have produced rigorous, evidence-based knowledge of great practical importance for policy, governance, and human welfare.
