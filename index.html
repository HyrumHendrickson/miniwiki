Mini Wiki is a personal encyclopedia devoted to the serious study of mathematics, classical history, philosophy, theology, and the broader inheritance of Western civilization. It is not intended to be a neutral compendium of all perspectives equally — it is written from within a tradition: one that regards reason and revelation as complementary, that honors the classical and Christian roots of Western thought, that takes seriously the American experiment in ordered liberty, and that draws on the restored gospel of Jesus Christ as taught by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

Articles are written for the curious reader who wants more than a summary — who wants to understand, to trace ideas to their sources, and to see how the great disciplines of human learning illuminate one another.

Mathematics

The mathematical articles on this site emphasize understanding over mere computation. Where possible, ideas are illustrated with interactive Desmos graphs and rendered with full LaTeX notation. The goal is to present mathematics as the ancient Greeks understood it: a discipline of pure reason, beautiful in itself, and indispensable to the natural philosopher.

Trigonometry
The geometry of angles and circles — from right-triangle ratios to the unit circle, identities, and the Fourier basis of all periodic phenomena.
Calculus
The mathematics of change and accumulation — limits, derivatives, integration, the Fundamental Theorem, and series.
Algebra
The language of structure and relationship — equations, polynomials, functions, and the abstract systems that unify them.

Classical History

Western civilization traces its political and philosophical inheritance directly to Greece and Rome. Athens bequeathed democracy, philosophy, and drama; Rome gave us law, the republic, and an imperial peace that transmitted Greek learning to the Christian world. These articles treat that legacy as a living possession, not a museum exhibit.

Athenian Democracy
The world's first experiment in self-government — its institutions, its philosophers, its contradictions, and its enduring influence on modern politics.
The Roman Republic
Five centuries of republican government, senatorial virtue, and constitutional innovation that directly shaped the American founders.

American History

The United States represents the most sustained attempt in human history to build a government on the principles of natural law, consent of the governed, and individual liberty secured by constitutional order. These articles treat the American founding not as mythology but as a serious political achievement — one worth understanding, defending, and transmitting.

The American Founding
The intellectual origins, revolutionary struggle, and constitutional architecture of the United States of America.
The Civil War
The great crisis of the republic — its causes, its conduct, and its resolution in an expanded understanding of American liberty.

Philosophy

Philosophy, properly understood, is not merely academic — it is the love of wisdom, the disciplined use of reason in pursuit of truth. These articles draw primarily on the classical and Christian philosophical tradition: Aristotle, Aquinas, the natural law theorists, and their modern inheritors.

Logic
The formal study of valid inference — from Aristotle's syllogistic and the laws of thought to propositional and predicate logic.
Virtue Ethics
Aristotle's account of the good life — the virtues, practical wisdom, the mean, and the role of habit in forming character.
Natural Law
The tradition holding that moral and political norms are grounded in the rational nature of the human person and knowable by reason.
Christian Humanism
The synthesis of classical learning and Christian faith that drove the Renaissance and shaped Western education for five centuries.

Theology

These articles approach doctrine seriously and from within belief. They draw primarily on the theology of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints — presenting restored-gospel doctrine as a coherent and intellectually serious account of the nature of God, man, and eternity.

The Plan of Salvation
The Latter-day Saint account of the pre-mortal life, the purpose of mortality, the Atonement, and the eternal destiny of the human soul.
The Restoration
The historical and doctrinal account of the restoration of the gospel, the priesthood, and the Church in the nineteenth century.
A Note on Perspective

This wiki does not pretend to the false neutrality claimed by institutional encyclopedias. Articles are written from within a tradition that honors the Western inheritance, takes classical and Christian philosophy seriously as intellectual achievements, regards the American constitutional order as a genuine political good, and presents Latter-day Saint doctrine from a position of faith. Readers who disagree with any of these commitments are welcome — honest engagement with a clearly stated position is more useful than studied vagueness.
