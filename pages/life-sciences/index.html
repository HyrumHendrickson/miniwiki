Life Sciences

Life Sciences
ScopeStudy of living organisms
Major disciplinesCell Biology, Genetics, Evolution, Ecology, Human Anatomy
MethodObservation, experimentation, microscopy, molecular analysis

The life sciences investigate the structures, functions, growth, origin, evolution, and distribution of living organisms. From the molecular machinery inside a single bacterium to the complex web of interactions that sustains a tropical rainforest, life scientists seek to understand the principles that unite all living things and the mechanisms that have produced the extraordinary diversity of life on Earth. The life sciences encompass a wide range of disciplines — from cell biology and genetics at the molecular end to ecology and evolutionary biology at the organismal and planetary end — unified by the twin foundations of cell theory and evolutionary theory.
