Philosophy
TraditionClassical and Christian Western philosophy
Primary sourcesPlato, Aristotle, Cicero, Aquinas, Augustine
ApproachReason in service of truth; faith and reason as complementary

Philosophy is the love of wisdom — the disciplined use of reason to pursue truth about God, man, and the world. The articles here draw primarily on the classical and Christian philosophical tradition: the Greek philosophers who first systematized logic and ethics, the Stoics who universalized natural law, the medieval synthesis of Augustine and Aquinas, and the Renaissance humanists who united classical learning with Christian faith. This tradition is treated not as one option among many but as the most sustained and fruitful exercise of philosophical reason in the history of civilization.
