American History

American History
ScopeUnited States, colonial era to present
PerspectiveAmerican exceptionalism; constitutional order as genuine achievement
Philosophical rootNatural law, classical republicanism, Protestant Christianity

The United States is not merely one nation among others — it is the most ambitious attempt in history to found a government explicitly on the principles of natural law, individual rights, and constitutional restraint. The American experiment deserves to be taken seriously on its own terms: as a political achievement of the first order, imperfect in execution but remarkable in aspiration and durability.

Articles in this category treat American history from a perspective of measured appreciation for the constitutional tradition — neither uncritical hagiography nor the cynical revisionism that reduces the founders to their worst contradictions. The Declaration's claim that all men are created equal was a moral promise that the nation has spent centuries learning to fulfill; that process is itself one of the great stories of political history.
